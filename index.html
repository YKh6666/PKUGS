Why PKU-GS

Designed for Research on Large-Scene Gaussian Splatting Compression

The project page foregrounds the dataset properties that matter most for reproducible reconstruction, evaluation, and compression-focused analysis.

Large-Scale Scene Coverage

The dataset emphasizes wide-area reconstruction targets that remain challenging for scene representation and compression.

UAV-Oriented Capture Protocol

Acquisition is centered on aerial viewpoints, long trajectories, and practical flight patterns rather than synthetic camera paths.

Real-World Scene Diversity

Architectural detail, vegetation, reflective materials, and cluttered urban layouts introduce meaningful complexity.

Support for Compression Research

PKU-GS is designed to facilitate analysis of storage efficiency, reconstruction quality, and scene-level benchmarking.

Capture & Processing Pipeline

A Clear Pipeline from Aerial Acquisition to Compression Evaluation

The processing flow is presented as a compact research pipeline, emphasizing the transition from real UAV capture to benchmark-oriented Gaussian Splatting analysis.

Step 1

UAV Capture

Long-range UAV trajectories collect large-scene observations with practical aerial viewpoints.

Step 2

Video Preprocessing

Raw sequences are organized, filtered, and prepared for structured downstream reconstruction.

Step 3

Pose Estimation

Camera poses are recovered to establish geometric consistency across frames and viewpoints.

Step 4

Gaussian Splatting Reconstruction

Scene representations are reconstructed for rendering, analysis, and compression-oriented study.

Step 5

Compression Benchmark

Methods are compared with quality, efficiency, and scene-level robustness in mind.
